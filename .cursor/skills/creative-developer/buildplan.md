# BUILD_PLAN.md

# Baarish
## Production Plan

---

# Purpose of this Document

This document does not explain how the project should be built.

It explains what should exist after each stage of development.

Every phase should leave the experience feeling complete in itself while also preparing the foundation for the next stage.

No phase should introduce placeholder experiences.

Every completed milestone should feel like a meaningful step toward the final artwork.

The objective is not simply to complete features.

The objective is to slowly bring an entire world to life.

---

# Overall Development Philosophy

The project should always evolve from large ideas to small details.

Never begin with tiny interactions.

Never polish unfinished worlds.

Always establish the emotional foundation before introducing complexity.

The visitor should feel like the world already exists before individual characters begin inhabiting it.

Development should follow this natural order:

World

↓

Atmosphere

↓

Movement

↓

Life

↓

People

↓

Stories

↓

Details

↓

Polish

Everything built later should naturally belong to what already exists.

Nothing should ever feel attached afterwards.

---

# Phase 01

## Identity

### Goal

Create the project's identity before creating the experience itself.

At the end of this phase, someone opening the project should immediately understand the emotional tone without exploring the world.

---

### Visitor Experience

The visitor arrives in silence.

The atmosphere immediately feels calm.

Nothing moves aggressively.

Nothing asks for attention.

The project introduces itself with confidence.

The visitor understands they are about to experience something artistic rather than commercial.

The transition into the world should feel like opening the first page of a beautifully illustrated book.

---

### Deliverables

The project has a clear title.

The introductory screen establishes atmosphere.

Typography already reflects the emotional direction.

The transition into the world feels intentional.

Nothing distracts from the anticipation.

---

### Success Criteria

Someone should already understand the mood before interacting with anything.

---

# Phase 02

## Birth of the World

### Goal

Create the town itself.

No stories yet.

No emotional moments.

Only the living environment.

The visitor should believe this town exists independently of them.

---

### Visitor Experience

The first journey through the environment should already feel peaceful.

Roads naturally connect.

Buildings belong where they are.

Distances feel believable.

Nothing appears randomly placed.

The world should already invite exploration despite containing very little interaction.

---

### Deliverables

The complete town layout exists.

Roads.

Buildings.

Shops.

Trees.

Street furniture.

Electric poles.

Street lighting.

Balconies.

Side streets.

Open spaces.

Transitions between areas feel natural.

The entire journey exists inside one uninterrupted world.

There should never be a sense of disconnected scenes.

---

### Success Criteria

If every character disappeared, the world would still feel alive.

---

# Phase 03

## Atmosphere

### Goal

Transform the environment into monsoon.

Without changing the layout, completely transform how it feels emotionally.

The town should begin breathing.

---

### Visitor Experience

The visitor immediately notices weather before architecture.

Rain becomes the defining characteristic.

Visibility changes.

Reflections appear.

Fog softens distance.

Windows glow warmly.

Streetlights become meaningful.

The environment begins communicating emotion without needing people.

---

### Deliverables

Rain feels like part of the environment.

Lighting reacts naturally.

Reflections exist wherever appropriate.

The world begins feeling humid.

The atmosphere changes over time instead of remaining static.

Thunder occasionally reminds visitors that the storm continues beyond what they can see.

---

### Success Criteria

Someone should immediately recognize the season without reading a single word.

---

# Phase 04

## Camera Journey

### Goal

The visitor should stop feeling like they are scrolling a webpage.

They should begin feeling like they are travelling through a place.

---

### Visitor Experience

Movement becomes effortless.

The world moves.

Not the interface.

The camera naturally discovers spaces.

Every transition feels physically believable.

Nothing abruptly appears.

Nothing suddenly disappears.

The journey becomes continuous.

Visitors should quickly stop thinking about scrolling.

Instead they simply experience movement.

---

### Deliverables

Every location naturally flows into the next.

Movement never interrupts immersion.

Perspective changes subtly.

The camera behaves like a quiet observer.

---

### Success Criteria

Visitors should forget they are navigating a website.

---

# Phase 05

## Life Before People

### Goal

The environment begins living before characters arrive.

Life should emerge from small details.

---

### Visitor Experience

Even empty streets feel occupied.

Rainwater drips from roofs.

Leaves react to wind.

Puddles continuously ripple.

Steam rises from somewhere unseen.

Lights flicker.

Distant silhouettes occasionally appear.

Nothing remains perfectly still.

The visitor begins noticing details instead of searching for interaction.

---

### Deliverables

Environmental movement exists everywhere.

Every section contains subtle motion.

The world feels active even without human presence.

---

### Success Criteria

Standing still remains enjoyable.

The world never feels frozen.


# Phase 06

# Human Stories

## Goal

Introduce people into the world.

Not as characters in a story.

Not as interactive NPCs.

Not as animated assets.

They are ordinary people living ordinary lives.

The visitor should never feel that these people exist for entertainment.

Instead, the visitor should feel like they accidentally witnessed small moments from strangers' lives.

Every character should have a purpose.

Every emotion should feel genuine.

No character should exist simply to fill empty space.

---

## Visitor Experience

The visitor slowly begins noticing people.

Nobody waves.

Nobody acknowledges the visitor.

Nobody performs.

Life simply continues.

The visitor becomes an invisible observer.

Each person quietly reveals one relationship with the rain.

The experience becomes deeply human without relying on dialogue.

---

## Deliverables

Every major area contains meaningful human presence.

Each emotional moment feels unique.

No two characters communicate the same emotion.

Characters naturally belong to their surroundings.

The town begins feeling inhabited.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors begin remembering people instead of buildings.

---

# Phase 07

# Ghar
## Warmth

---

## Goal

The first emotional stop should immediately establish comfort.

This scene represents safety.

Home.

Warmth.

Togetherness.

---

## Visitor Experience

As the visitor approaches the house, the cold blue atmosphere slowly becomes warmer.

The glow from the windows feels inviting.

Inside, a family quietly enjoys the evening.

Fresh chai.

Pakodas.

Conversation.

Laughter.

Nothing dramatic happens.

Nothing extraordinary happens.

Yet this should become one of the most comforting moments of the journey.

The visitor should almost wish they could step inside.

---

## Deliverables

The home feels lived in.

Warm lighting softly contrasts the rainy outdoors.

Steam.

Curtains.

Reflections.

Subtle family movement.

Comfort without excess.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors should immediately understand that rain can also mean happiness.

---

# Phase 08

# Gali
## Childhood

---

## Goal

Represent the relationship between rain and childhood.

Not nostalgia for adults.

Actual childhood joy.

Pure.

Unfiltered.

---

## Visitor Experience

Children completely ignore inconvenience.

Rain becomes their playground.

Puddles become oceans.

Paper boats become adventures.

Laughter naturally fills the street.

Nearby, another child watches from inside a window.

Unable to join.

The contrast quietly introduces longing without breaking the joyful atmosphere.

The visitor experiences two completely different childhoods within the same street.

---

## Deliverables

Joy feels spontaneous.

Window child feels genuine.

Paper boats naturally drift.

Water splashes react believably.

Nothing feels choreographed.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors smile before they realize they are smiling.

---

# Phase 09

# Bus Stop
## Different Lives. Same Rain.

---

## Goal

Show that one event creates many different emotions.

No one is wrong.

Everyone has a reason.

---

## Visitor Experience

The visitor enters a bus stop where multiple strangers share the same shelter.

Each person silently tells a different story.

Someone is frustrated.

Someone feels relieved.

Someone worries about reaching home.

Someone unexpectedly grows closer to another person.

Nobody speaks.

Nobody explains.

Body language says everything.

The visitor naturally shifts attention between people, understanding each emotion without needing narration.

---

## Deliverables

Distinct emotional personalities.

Natural spacing.

Subtle interactions.

Believable waiting behaviour.

No exaggerated acting.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors begin empathizing with strangers.

---

# Phase 10

# Chai Tapri
## Community

---

## Goal

Create the emotional heart of the project.

This is where strangers temporarily become neighbours.

---

## Visitor Experience

Rain forces people under one roof.

Some wait.

Some drink chai.

Some quietly observe the rain.

Someone serves everyone.

A street dog peacefully sleeps nearby.

Steam slowly rises.

Warm light spills onto wet roads.

For a few moments, everyone belongs together.

The visitor should feel invited without ever becoming part of the scene.

---

## Deliverables

Warm lighting dominates.

Steam feels alive.

Small conversations naturally happen in the background.

The tea stall becomes visually unforgettable.

The dog remains peacefully indifferent.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors instinctively slow down here.

---

# Phase 12

# Office Window
## Distance

---

## Goal

End the emotional journey with longing.

Not sadness.

Simply acceptance.

---

## Visitor Experience

The visitor reaches an office building.

Inside, someone watches the rain through glass.

Unable to experience it.

Outside, life continues.

School children walk home.

The rain belongs to everyone.

Yet not everyone can enjoy it.

The visitor quietly understands the difference.

---

## Deliverables

Warm interior.

Cool exterior.

Glass reflections.

Subtle office movement.

Gentle ending.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors leave feeling reflective rather than emotional.

---

# Phase 13

# Sound

---

## Goal

Allow visitors to hear the world before they consciously notice it.

Sound should never explain.

Only deepen immersion.

---

## Visitor Experience

Rain exists everywhere.

Every location introduces subtle changes.

Children sound distant.

Tea stalls feel alive.

Thunder occasionally rolls across the town.

Vehicles pass somewhere outside the visible world.

The soundscape continuously changes as the visitor travels.

Silence remains equally important.

---

## Deliverables

Layered environmental audio.

Smooth transitions.

No repetitive loops.

Natural ambience.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors continue hearing the town after closing the website.

---

Dripping roofs.

Overflowing gutters.

Moving curtains.

# Phase 15

# Emotional Polish

---

## Goal

Remove anything that feels artificial.

Increase everything that feels human.

---

## Visitor Experience

Nothing feels rushed.

Nothing feels repetitive.

The journey naturally breathes.

Every transition feels earned.

Every pause feels intentional.

The experience becomes less about interaction and more about presence.

---

## Deliverables

Balanced pacing.

Consistent atmosphere.

Smooth emotional progression.

Natural timing.

No visual distractions.

---

## Success Criteria

Visitors never feel overwhelmed.

---

# Phase 16

# Final World Review

---

## Goal

Treat the project as a real place instead of software.

Walk through every part of the town asking one question:

"Would this still feel believable if nobody visited today?"

---

## Review Questions

Does every building have purpose?

Does every object belong?

Does every person have a reason to be there?

Does every light source make sense?

Does every pause improve the experience?

Does every movement feel alive?

Does every sound belong?

Does every emotional moment feel earned?

Does every transition preserve immersion?

---

## Success Criteria

Nothing exists because it looks cool.

Everything exists because it belongs.

---

# Phase 17

# Completion

---

## Goal

Leave visitors with a feeling they cannot easily describe.

Not because the project is complicated.

Because it is honest.

---

## Final Visitor Experience

The visitor slowly reaches the end.

Nothing dramatic happens.

The rain continues.

Life continues.

The town does not stop existing.

The visitor simply leaves.

The world quietly remains.

---

## What People Should Remember

Not the animations.

Not the graphics.

Not the technology.

Not the interactions.

They should remember:

A child waiting at the window.

A couple sharing an umbrella.

A tea stall glowing in the rain.

A paper boat floating away.

A dog sleeping peacefully.

The warmth inside a home.

The silence of an office window.

The feeling of walking through rain without getting wet.

---

# Final Success

If someone closes the website and immediately sends it to a friend saying,

> "You have to experience this."

then the project has succeeded.

Not because it is technically impressive.

But because, for a few quiet minutes,

it made them feel like they were home.
