# STORY.md

# बारिश
## Ek Nazar. Kai Ehsaas.

---

## The Story

There isn't a hero in this story.

There isn't a villain either.

There is only one rain.

The same rain falls on every street, every house, every window and every person. Yet somehow, everyone experiences it differently.

This experience follows that idea.

The visitor enters a small North Indian town during a monsoon evening and slowly travels through it. There are no missions, no objectives and no interactions that interrupt the journey. The visitor simply observes life unfolding around them.

Nothing in this world exists to impress the visitor. Life continues whether someone is watching or not.

As the journey progresses, the visitor slowly discovers that rain means something different to every individual.

---

# Beginning

The experience begins before the town is visible.

The screen is dark.

Only the sound of rain can be heard.

Not heavy rain.

Just enough to immediately transport the visitor into a rainy evening.

After a few quiet moments, the title appears.

"बारिश"

Below it, a single line appears.

"Ek Nazar. Kai Ehsaas."

The title slowly fades away.

Instead of revealing everything at once, the world slowly appears through rain and mist, almost as if the visitor is opening their eyes inside this evening.

The town is already alive.

Nobody waits for the visitor.

Nobody notices that someone has arrived.

The journey has already begun.

---

# The First Look

The visitor enters from one end of the town.

The first thing they notice is not people.

It is the atmosphere.

The wet roads.

Streetlights reflecting in puddles.

Old houses.

Scooters parked outside.

Telephone wires cutting across the sky.

Plants growing through cracked walls.

Warm yellow lights glowing from windows.

Rain gently falling over everything.

The visitor should immediately feel that this is a real place.

Not a movie set.

Not an illustration.

A place where people actually live.

---

# Scene One
## Home

The first emotional moment is a home.

Through a slightly open window, a family can be seen sitting together.

Someone is making fresh chai.

Pakodas are placed on the table.

People are laughing.

Someone is looking outside while holding a cup of tea.

The rain has made everyone stay inside, but nobody minds.

In fact, this rain has created this moment.

The visitor doesn't hear every conversation.

They only understand the feeling.

For this family, rain means comfort.

Rain means slowing down.

Rain means being together.

The visitor quietly continues their journey.

---

# Scene Two
## The Street

The visitor leaves the warmth of the house and enters the street outside.

Life immediately becomes more energetic.

Children are running through puddles without caring about getting wet.

One child jumps into every puddle they can find.

Another carefully places a paper boat into flowing rainwater and follows it as it floats away.

Nearby, another child watches everything from inside a window.

Their hand rests against the glass.

They want to go outside.

Their parents have told them they cannot.

Nothing dramatic happens.

The visitor simply experiences two different childhoods existing only a few metres apart.

For one child, rain is freedom.

For another, it is waiting.

---

# Scene Three
## The Bus Stop

The visitor continues moving until they reach a crowded bus stop.

Everyone stands under the same roof.

Everyone looks at the same rain.

Yet nobody feels the same thing.

One office worker looks frustrated.

Their clothes are soaked.

They are already late.

Another person quietly smiles because the unbearable summer heat has finally disappeared.

Someone else repeatedly checks the road, hoping the bus arrives soon.

Nearby, a young couple shares one umbrella.

Without saying much, they slowly move closer together.

Rain has delayed everyone's journey.

But for two people, it has created a beautiful memory.

The visitor slowly realizes that rain never changes.

Only perspective changes.

---

# Scene Four
## Chai Tapri

The road leads to a small tea stall glowing with warm yellow light.

This place feels different.

People who don't know each other stand together simply because the rain has brought them under the same roof.

Some quietly sip chai.

Some watch vehicles pass.

Some simply enjoy the weather.

The chaiwala works continuously.

Today is a good day for business.

Steam rises from freshly poured tea.

The smell almost feels real.

Under a nearby bench, a street dog sleeps peacefully.

It has found the perfect place to wait for the rain to slow down.

Nothing extraordinary happens here.

Yet somehow, this becomes one of the most comforting places in the journey.

The visitor naturally slows down.

Nobody wants to rush past this moment.

---

# Scene Five
## The Open Road

Leaving the tea stall behind, the town becomes quieter.

The roads become wider.

There are fewer people.

Rain feels louder here.

A single person walks alone without an umbrella.

Instead of running for shelter, they quietly smile.

Nobody is watching.

For a few seconds, they simply enjoy getting drenched.

Further ahead, an auto driver waits for passengers.

The weather is pleasant now.

Business is slow.

For one person, rain has become freedom.

For another, it has become uncertainty.

The visitor begins reflecting on their own relationship with rain.

---

# Scene Six
## Office Window

The final stop of the journey is an office building.

Inside, someone stands near a large window.

They watch the rain while continuing to work.

Outside, children walk home.

People continue their evening.

Life continues.

The woman smiles slightly before returning to her desk.

Not because she dislikes the rain.

Because today, she cannot be part of it.

Sometimes rain isn't something we experience.

Sometimes it is simply something we watch.

The visitor quietly understands this feeling.

---

# Ending

There is no dramatic ending.

No final message appears.

No lesson is explained.

The visitor slowly reaches the end of the town.

Behind them, everything continues.

Children still play.

Tea is still being served.

The bus eventually arrives.

The family continues talking.

The office lights remain on.

The dog is still asleep.

The rain keeps falling.

The visitor leaves.

The town doesn't.

---

# Final Thought

This experience is not about rain.

Rain is only the thread that connects every moment.

The real story has always been about people.

About how one ordinary evening can contain hundreds of different emotions.

About how the same rain can become joy for someone, comfort for another, frustration for someone else and a memory for someone who simply watches it through a window.

The visitor doesn't finish this experience with an answer.

They leave with a question.

"What does rain mean to me?"
